Short bio:

Ulf Aslak (b. 1990) is a Danish physicist, data scientist and creative coder. He studies human mobility, social dynamics and other complex phenomena.

CV

I am a Senior Data Scientist at Danske Bank in the Advanced Analytics department. Formerly a PostDoc at DTU Compute in Sune Lehmann's group working on the HOPE project, assistant professor at the Center for Social Data Science and external lecturer at DIS Copenhagen. I have published research on human mobility, community detection in temporal networks, user behavior in online platforms, and computational fluid dynamics (early career). I maintain multiple open source Python libraries, notably Infostop and Netwulf.

I have a PhD in Complex Systems and Social Data, and hold degrees in Human Centered AI (MSc) and Physics (BSc) from DTU. I have been a visiting researcher in the Uri Alon lab at the Weizmann Institute of Science (Israel), and in the Brockmann Lab at the Robert Koch Institute (Germany), and attended the Complex Systems Summer School at the Santa Fe Institute (USA). On a number of occasions I have provided consultancy for private and public companies.

On a practical note, I am an expert Python and JavaScript programmer; and occasional C++ user. I build lots of data visualizations for the web using tools like Observable, D3.js, Hugo, and Svelte. I use Python for most data processing and scientific computing, and C++ for speeding things up.
