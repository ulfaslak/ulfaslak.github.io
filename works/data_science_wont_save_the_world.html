On doing.

"2021"

Let me preface the following by apologizing for its banality. Nothing I state below is in any way novel or exciting. It is the kind of truth you carry so close to you that you are not even conscious of it. Or it is an idea you have but neglected to commit to memory because its simplicty makes it seem unimportant. And maybe it is to you. But not to me. To me it is the single most important wisdom and I hold on to it because not understanding it in the past has caused me tremendous pain. It is the stoic advice: If you want to feel good, do good things. I find examples everywhere that supports this wisdom. It explains a deep and painful frustration that always followed big successes in my life. Hard work culminating in grades, certificates, defences, applauses and even job offers. I was always numb to these successes but I kept working towards them. It wasn't until I had taken the last carrot education could offer that I really understood what my problem was. I had never truly done anything. And worse, since I had put my energy into obtaining external validation of my capabilities I had not prioritized the most important thing: doing those things I was good at. Because it never felt like doing. In truth, the only moments where I felt truly happy with my job was when I were building things. Software mostly.
