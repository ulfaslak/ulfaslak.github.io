Recent Work

[Visualisation] Communities in temporal networks

Interactive visualisation, enabling users to explore social patterns of students at the Technical University of Denmark. The project was done in collaboration with Sensible DTU as an attempt to model the dynamical structure of communities in a temporal network of human interactions. Developed with D3.js.

[Animation] Membrane Testing System Tutorial

A basic animated tutorial showing how to start, shutdown, or re-start Sterlitech's Sepa CF Membrane Testing System. The animation was developed, narrated and produced by Ulf and directed in collaboration with the Sterlitech Corporation.

[Machine Learning] Review fraud detection

A first attempt at using machine learning to mimick human fraud detection on the Trustpilot review service. The project was carried out in collaboration with Trustpilot and used data from over 17 million reviews. The study finds that fraud detection can be automated with 96.6 % accuracy using a Random Forest classification model.

[Visualisation] Pointing Out Bias in Movie Critics

Exploratory visual analysis seeking to test the credibility of movie critics and understand just how predictable they are. The study is based on a dataset consisting of 947 critics, 17275 movies and 301409 reviews scraped from Rotten Tomatoes, and finds that movie critics are mostly biased towards liking small distributions and arty genres.

[Animation] Sterlitech Sepa CF RO Cell

3D Animation showing operation of the Sterlitech Sepa CF Reverse Osmosis membrane testing module. The polluted brown fluid is pressurized against a membrane inside the module, hence driving a permeate of clean water through the membrane and into the empty reservoir.

[Data Mining] Predicting the Danish Parliament

Web service that allows users to get instant predictions on outcomes of elections in the Danish parliament. The service was designed to give predictions for votes from each member of parliament. This was implemented by scraping voting statistics for all subjects and training classifiers for each, which included numerous motion/bill specific information including topics, obtained through topic mining using the Python topic mining implementation 'gensim'.

[Machine Learning] Why do people follow bots?

Study to detect general features present in human Twitter users that were more likely to connect and interact with bots than others. The data for this study was collected from social interactions, by inventing a female user with a particular interest in veganism and fitness. The bot turned out relatively popular generating 2500+ followers over the course of a month. The study revealed that features such as low friend reciprocity, happier usage of words in tweets and shorter time associated with Twitter were predicators for high bot susceptibility.

[Thesis] CFD Study of Osmotic Membrane Cells

Bachelor Thesis written in collaboration with DTU and Cleantech company Aquaporin A/S. The study sought to further the understanding of the influence of various geometrical parameters in membrane testing modules, on water transport across the membrane. The approach was purely computational employing CFD simulations using the open source framework OpenFOAM. The study concluded that various geometric aspects should be reconsidered in order to achieve more accurate results in commercial membrane testing modules.

Project/Startup Ideas

[Mobile App] "Litter": Location based video sharing

An app that lets you post videos to the locations where they were recorded. The videos can be watched by anyone passing by and could potentially be massively popular among urban sports performers of street artists wanting to make their mark on specific locations. Over time the generated data could be useful to understand how a city is used. Also, users looking for specific activities could simply filter searches by tags and know e.g. where parkour or skateboarding was popular.

[Web community] Safely validating startup ideas

Good ideas are nothing without proper feedback. What if there existed a platform where users could safely share their best ideas and get feedback from other inventors? A place that ensured you ownership over ideas posted, and where fellow users were validated through Stack Exchange reputation or the like. Any great idea would float to the surface through upvotes and modification, until it was solid enough to form the basis of a startup, led by yourself or someone you trust through the site.

[App/Technology] Smartlock bike taxi

This idea is inspired by Skylock and Uber. Imagine that you have a bike lock that you can control through an app, almost like the Skylock. When you don't care to use the bike yourself, you can put it somewhere in the city and allow other users of the app to ride it against a charge. Users in need of a bike can check the app to see the location of nearby bikes locked with the smartlock. This is essentially the driverless Uber for bikes. Someone is now doing this.

[Mobile app] A shoppinglist with integrated offers

This is a half finished project that could have easily turned into a startup. Essentially, it is just another shopping list app, but using the API from eTilbudsavis it is possible to integrate offers in the shopping list, such that users can know exactly where to get their groceries at the best price. Was developed as a proof of concept prototype in iOS 8 with all the essential functionality, which worked well enough to excite several test subjects. Aside from offering price-conscious consumers a useful tool, this app also has great potential for monetization. Click the image for report.
